Van Gogh's Gallery

Vincent Willem van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist whose work highly influenced 20th-century art. He's created approximately 2100 artworks, including 860 oil paintings. The artist struggled with mental illness and remained poor and virtually unknown throughout his life, but nowadays is considered one of the greatest painters of his era.

  • 30 March, 1853

    Vincent Van Gogh was born in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands.

  • 1 May, 1857

    The birth of Theo, Vincent's dear brother.

  • 1869

    Recommended by his uncle, Van Gogh started working at Goupil & Cie, an art dealership, in the Hague.

  • 1873

    Vincent was transferred to the London's Groupil Gallery.

  • 1879

    Van Gogh becomes a missionary at coal-mining district of Borinage in Belgium.

  • 1880

    The artist registered at Académie Royale des Beaux-Arts, started studying anatomy, modelling and perspective.

  • 1885

    Van Gogh finished his first masterpiece, The Potato Eaters.

  • 1888

    Van Gogh moved to Arles. During his time in Arles, he produced approximately 200 paintings and 100 drawings, including Cafe Terrace at Night, Starry Night Over the Rhone and Sunflowers.

  • 1889

    The artist is admitted to St Remy asylum. During his stay Van Gogh created one of his most known works, Starry Night.

  • 29 July 1890

    Van Gogh died in Auvers-sur-Oise, France, aged 37.
